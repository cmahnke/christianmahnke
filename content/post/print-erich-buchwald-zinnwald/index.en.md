Erich Buchwald-Zinnwald (1884–1972) was a landscape painter and graphic artist, best known for his colour woodcuts of the Eastern Ore Mountains. Out of a sense of attachment to this region, he also adopted the surname "Zinnwald". He was born in Dresden and moved to West Germany in 1968; from then on, he was regarded in the former GDR as a "[Republikflüchtling](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republikfl%C3%BCchtling)" (refugee from the Republic).

The title of the print is "Dresden around 1900"; it dates from 1952 and is inscribed with "Original woodcut" and the signature "E. Buchwald Zinnwald". A similar woodcut by Buchwald-Zinnwald is listed at [MutualArt](https://www.mutualart.com/Artwork/Dresden-um-1900/24933BACCE22E3130432FD02A4BE7CD9).
