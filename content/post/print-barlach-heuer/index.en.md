Barlach Heuer was a painter, graphic artist and glass collector from Elmshorn. He spent many years of his life in Paris, where he also died. The first name "Barlach" probably stems from a family connection to the sculptor Ernst Barlach.

The Elmshorn Art Association dedicated a commemorative exhibition to him in [2020](https://www.kunstverein-elmshorn.de/barlach-heuer-zum-gedenken/) (German).

The title of the print is “[Ygdrasil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yggdrasil)”, it is a colour woodcut made from two blocks and dates from 1971. It is listed in [“Farbdrucke” by the artist, published in 1980 by Nacke in Wuppertal](https://d-nb.info/810221586) (ISBN: 3-920365-01-1).
