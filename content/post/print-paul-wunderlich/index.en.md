Paul Wunderlich studied at the Hamburg University of Fine Arts from 1947 (alongside, for example, [Horst Janssen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horst_Janssen)). From 1963 to 1968, he was a professor at the [HfbK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_bildende_K%C3%BCnste_Hamburg) in Hamburg.

He is particularly known for his erotic to mystical-looking depictions; his style can be described as [Surrealism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surrealism), a style he developed from around 1959. In 1960, a series of his lithographs was confiscated by the Hamburg Public Prosecutor’s Office on grounds of "obscene depictions". 

The [Paul Wunderlich House](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul-Wunderlich-Haus) (German) has a further print in its [collection](https://www.paul-wunderlich-haus.de/stiftungssammlung/1946-2/?ggt_2907=Holzschnitt&ggs_2907=lesende). The print does not appear in his catalogue raisonné, as this only documents his work from 1948 onwards; the print is dated 1946.
