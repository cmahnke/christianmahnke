Hermann Zapf (1918–2015) was a globally influential German typographer and calligrapher who created works such as this print. He initially completed an apprenticeship as a photo retoucher and subsequently taught himself typography.
He not only designed well-known typefaces such as [Optima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optima), [Palatino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palatino) and [Zapfino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapfino), but also the best-known set of decorative characters, [Zapf Dingbats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapf_Dingbats).

The saying *"One must multiply the number of thoughts to such an extent that there are not enough guardians for them"* comes from [Jerzy Lec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Jerzy_Lec), a Polish aphorist. The text is written in a handwriting reminiscent of the Zapfino typeface.
