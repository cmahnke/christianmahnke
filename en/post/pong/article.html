Pong Visualisation

A 70s video game as a heat map

For some reason I came up with the idea of visualising Pong1…

I have a small collection of old game consoles from before 1980, many2 of which are based on the AY-3-8500 chip3. The most famous game made possible by this chip is Pong.

Research

Now of course you could make countless games, record them, digitise them and then visualise them, but that would take a long time, especially as you would also need two people, as the old systems do not allow a game against the “computer”. Therefore, a programmable simulation was needed. I came across the following interesting projects and sites during my research:

  • PONG story: Great site about Pong and early consoles4

    • PONG Story: Pong for the PC5

  • OdysseyNow6 - Based on Unity

  • Pong on the Arduino

    • 70’s TV game recreation using an Arduino (Atmel ATmega328/168) processor.7 - Extensive documentation for a faithful recreation
    • Pong for Arduino8 - one of many other examples

  • JavaScript implementation

    • Javascript Pong by Jake Gordon9 - see below

  • Other

    • Pong Consoles Simulation10

    • POS (Pong Consoles) CPUs and Other Chips11 in the Emulation General Wiki12

Implementation

The image was generated on the basis of typescript-pong13. This project is a fork (or TypeScript port) of javascript-pong14. The documentation15 for the project is very detailed, but the TypeScript variant is easier to customise. Basically, only infrastructural changes, e.g. a non-interactive mode, were necessary.

This required a simple test based on Playwright16. This allows the recording of the games in the browser and the download of the recording to be automated.

Post-processing

A few post-processing steps are necessary to generate the image based on the recordings:

  • Add video frames together, inspired by python-image-averaging17
  • Separate static and dynamic image parts
  • Gamma correction of the dynamic areas to emphasise differences, blur, then colourise
  • merge areas again

OpenCV18 and NumPy19 are used for the image operations, Matplotlib20 for the colouring.

Visualisation

Pong Heatmap

In the picture, the static areas are white, as in the game; the coloured areas show how often something happens in which part of the picture.

The distribution follows this scale: On the left (cyan) little activity, on the right (magenta) a lot of activity.

Statistics

The image consists of 1777136 individual images, or 2:42 hours of gameplay, or 333 rounds.
