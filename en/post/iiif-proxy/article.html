IIIF Proxy

On-the-fly image processing for IIIF resources
Wednesday, January 26, 2022
Christian Mahnke0009-0003-1361-4159
URL: https://christianmahnke.de/en/post/iiif-proxy/
Keywords:

To improve the ‘displayability’ of digital objects such as coins and books, I have written a proxy for IIIF resources…

The digital collections of the University of Göttingen are mainly fed from two portals:

  • Scientific Collections of the Georg-August-Universität Göttingen1
  • Göttingen Digitisation Centre2

Both make their digitised material available via IIIF3. Unfortunately, the images provided are not optimised for aesthetically pleasing secondary use.

In order to minimise manual post-processing and the resulting storage effort, I have slightly extended an existing IIIF server - Hymir from MDZ4 - so that it is able to sit on an institutional server and process the images delivered by it.

In order to keep the image processing configurable, a field of the IIIF Image API is used to specify the operation. The quality5 field was suitable for this, the proxy allows:

  • nofold - remove page fold
  • transparent-background - Transparent background
  • nofold+transparentbg - both together

For the image operations themselves, OpenCV6 is used, whereby the background is simply removed with a threshold value and only at the edges - i.e. made transparent. Recognising the folds is a little more complicated, but it’s not rocket surgery either:

  • Recognising lines ( Probabilistic Hough Line Transform7) in the fold area depending on whether they are left or right-sided
  • Sorting the recognised lines by angle and length to identify the most likely candidate of the fold
  • Rotate to align the detected fold vertically

But here the reliability could be improved.
