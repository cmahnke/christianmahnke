"Lesende" (Reading woman) by Paul Wunderlich (1927-2010)

Saturday, August 3, 2024

I’ve found a linocut by Paul Wunderlich (1927–2010) (on artnet)…

Print
Signature

Paul Wunderlich studied at the Hamburg University of Fine Arts from 1947 (alongside, for example, Horst Janssen). From 1963 to 1968, he was a professor at the HfbK in Hamburg.

He is particularly known for his erotic to mystical-looking depictions; his style can be described as Surrealism, a style he developed from around 1959. In 1960, a series of his lithographs was confiscated by the Hamburg Public Prosecutor’s Office on grounds of “obscene depictions”.

The Paul Wunderlich House (German) has a further print in its collection. The print does not appear in his catalogue raisonné, as this only documents his work from 1948 onwards; the print is dated 1946.
